About Me

Born in Santa Clara, Cuba, Andy William Duarte Durán developed a deep passion for painting from a very young age, which led him to explore the world of art through studies with various private teachers.

His dedication and talent led him to enroll at the Oscar Fernández Morera Professional Art School in Trinidad, Cuba, where he completed his final year at the Benny Moré Academy in Cienfuegos, consolidating his skills and expanding his artistic vision.

During his training, Andy participated in multiple exhibitions that allowed him to share his work with a wider audience and receive valuable feedback on his art.

After completing his studies, he served two years of military service and later enrolled at the Higher Institute of Art in Havana, where he earned a degree in Conservation and Restoration of Cultural Heritage.

He had the opportunity to share his passion and knowledge as a professor at his alma mater's Paper and Photography Restoration Workshop, guiding future restorers and artists in the preservation of cultural heritage.

His work at the Cuban Fund for Cultural Assets as a decorator also allowed him to explore his creativity through projects aimed at strengthening his country's cultural identity.

Throughout his career, Andy has participated in various artistic initiatives and restoration projects, standing out for his commitment to heritage conservation. His approach to teaching and practice reflects an unwavering dedication to art and cultural values, values he continues to promote in each of his projects.
