A Space Where Art Comes to Life
Welcome to My Creative World

Welcome to my virtual studio, a place where a passion for color and form is transformed into unique works of art. My name is Andy William Duarte Durán, and I am a professional visual artist with a profound calling for creation and teaching.

My journey as a painting teacher has allowed me to guide countless creative souls, but my most personal work resides on the canvas. Additionally, my specialization as a restorer of heritage artworks on paper has granted me an intimate understanding of classical techniques and an immense respect for the durability and legacy of art. This dedication and technical knowledge are the soul of every painting I create for you.

Have you been inspired by my catalog? Wonderful! Each painting you see is just the beginning of what we can create together. My works are never pre-made; each piece is a conversation between your vision and my hands, painted exclusively for you once you place your order.

Imagine that empty wall coming to life with a work of art designed for your space, adapting not only the size and details but also the emotion you wish to evoke.

Each piece is 100% original, hand-painted in oil on canvas. These are not prints; your painting will have the rich texture and unparalleled quality that only an authentic work of art can offer.

The price will be personally tailored, based not only on the size but also on the complexity of the work, ensuring a piece that you will not only love but that will also fit your budget.

About Me


Born in Santa Clara, Cuba, Andy William Duarte Durán developed a deep passion for painting from a very young age, which led him to explore the world of art through studies with various private teachers.

His dedication and talent led him to enroll at the Oscar Fernández Morera Professional Art School in Trinidad, Cuba, where he completed his final year at the Benny Moré Academy in Cienfuegos, consolidating his skills and expanding his artistic vision.

During his training, Andy participated in multiple exhibitions that allowed him to share his work with a wider audience and receive valuable feedback on his art.

After completing his studies, he served two years of military service and later enrolled at the Higher Institute of Art in Havana, where he earned a degree in Conservation and Restoration of Cultural Heritage.

He had the opportunity to share his passion and knowledge as a professor at his alma mater's Paper and Photography Restoration Workshop, guiding future restorers and artists in the preservation of cultural heritage.

His work at the Cuban Fund for Cultural Assets as a decorator also allowed him to explore his creativity through projects aimed at strengthening his country's cultural identity.

Throughout his career, Andy has participated in various artistic initiatives and restoration projects, standing out for his commitment to heritage conservation. His approach to teaching and practice reflects an unwavering dedication to art and cultural values, values he continues to promote in each of his projects.
